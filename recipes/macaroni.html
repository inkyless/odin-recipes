Mac and Cheese

For a thicker mac and cheese, double the amount of macaroni. You can never have enough cheese, and I usually add an extra 1/2 cup without ruining the recipe. Keep a careful eye on the milk when boiling, as milk boils fast and burns easily.

    Ingredients
  • 8 ounces uncooked elbow macaroni
  • ¼ cup salted butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 ½ cups milk, or more as needed
  • 2 cups shredded sharp Cheddar cheese
  • ½ cup finely grated Parmesan cheese
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
    Steps
  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Make the macaroni and cheese
  3. Melt 1/4 cup butter in a medium skillet over low heat
  4. Gradually whisk 2 1/2 cups milk into the flour mixture, and bring to a simmer.
  5. Stir in shredded Cheddar and finely grated Parmesan cheeses; season with salt and pepper
  6. Pour cheese sauce over macaroni and stir until well combined.
  7. Make the bread crumb topping
  8. Spread bread crumbs over macaroni and cheese, then sprinkle with paprika.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven until topping is golden brown and macaroni and cheese is bubbling, about 30 minutes.
